The value of an unhurried morning

There is a particular kind of clarity that arrives before the day begins asking for things. It appears in a room with the windows open, in the first cup of coffee, in the small stretch of time with nowhere else to be.

An unhurried morning is not always a long one. Sometimes it is only ten minutes kept free from the pull of messages, lists, and noise. But even a little space changes the texture of a day.

It is easy to mistake speed for momentum. The slower beginning offers something else: attention. We notice what we are thinking, what we have been avoiding, and what is quietly asking to be made.

Perhaps the most useful thing a morning can give us is not productivity, but a chance to meet ourselves before the world does.
